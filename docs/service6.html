Psychological in mental health

Psychology, at its core, is the scientific study of the human mind and behavior. It is a multifaceted discipline, encompassing various sub-fields such as cognitive, developmental, clinical, and social psychology, each focusing on different aspects of human thought and action. This broad field aims to understand how and why we think, feel, and behave the way we do, utilizing empirical methods to study conscious and unconscious phenomena.

Humanistic Psychology

Humanistic psychology centers on the individual’s potential and stresses the importance of growth and self-actualization. This approach views people as inherently good and capable of self-healing and personal growth. It emphasizes a person-centered approach, where the therapist provides an environment of empathy, authenticity, and unconditional positive regard. This supportive setting enables individuals to explore their feelings and thoughts without fear of judgment, facilitating personal growth and self-understanding.
